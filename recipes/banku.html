Banku Recipe

Home

Description

Banku is a traditional Ghanaian dish made from fermented corn and cassava dough, cooked into a smooth, elastic dough. It is commonly served with okro soup, pepper sauce, or soup and fish.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups corn dough
  • 1 cup cassava dough
  • Water (as needed)
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Mix corn dough and cassava dough in a large pot. Add water gradually to form a smooth, thick paste.
  2. Place the pot on medium heat and stir continuously with a wooden spatula to prevent lumps.
  3. As the mixture thickens, add more water as needed and keep stirring until it becomes smooth and stretchy.
  4. Add salt to taste and continue stirring until fully cooked (about 20-30 minutes).
  5. Shape the banku into balls and serve hot with your choice of soup, stew, or pepper sauce and fish.
